My story so far...

After more than a decade working in infrastructure manufacturing: downsizing and the closure of my place of work led to unemployment.
As I looked to re-enter the work force I decided that while scary and a bit uncertain, a career change was what I desired. A new and exciting chapter in my life! But what do I really love to do?

One area of interest I have always enjoyed and excelled at was problem solving. Be it solving production or scheduling issues, understanding learning styles in order to train new employees or learning how to program a PLC to improve a process.
Another area of interest has been computers and technology. Here are some of the things I have done in the past for the fun and challenge but also for work related needs:

  • made simple programs and games in Basic (TRS-80, C=64 and Windows)
  • used HTML to create websites for personal hobbies like sim racing and guitars (notepad, just notepad)
  • learned enough PLC logic to reprogram limit switches in a manufacturing line (rslogix if I recall)
  • setup private internet facing servers to play games with friends and family (MySQL, SQL Server 2017)
  • made a virtual version of my living room in VR (Unity, Unreal Engine, Blender
  • created and updated detailed linked spreadsheets to track and report efficiencies and KPIs (Excel)

Thoughout my career and personal life I have had others say things like, "You should be in IT." or ,"Why are you not in computer programming?" and I would often say, "I just do it for fun", or "I don't have a compter science degree".


Wait a minute... Am I on to something here?
